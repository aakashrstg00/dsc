The Game None Heeds, But the world needs!

ABOUT

The main aim of this event series is to give exposure about the various competitions (Hackathons, Ideathons and Competitive coding) at college, national and international levels and to build confidence of the students to take up and WIN at these events!

What's more? LOADS of prizes for every challenge!
💸💸💸

HOW WILL IT WORK?

There will be a new challenge each week. Every challenge will be unique but the rules will be the same:

  1. A fixed deadline (Cause everyone loves a race against time!🔥)
  2. Prizes for the best
  3. A practical and credible idea
  4. A solid team of 2 - 4 members (you can be a lone wolf too)
  5. Extra points for performing special tasks that will be unique for each challenge

The Leaderboard COMING SOON

The Almighty Leaderboard remembers everything. The scores of all teams will be recorded and displayed here and the performances of every team will be tracked. There will be a grand prize for consistently top teams.

FAQs

It's an online intra college event. There are 3 genres:

Ideathon -
You need to give a presentation solution for one of the problem statements. Do market research and give a business model.
Hackathon -
In this, you need to build a minimum viable product (app/hardware/anything else) for the problem statement.
CodeSprint-
This will be a competitive programming challenge.

There will be multiple challenges throughout the semester.

Id#01

"The Season begins"

The first ever challenge in the series is designed keeping in mind the beginners. The challenge is simple and easy to implement.


PRIZE POOL - ₹2019


Themes:

The themes are decided by the problem statements, so currently there are 3 themes -

  • College Life
  • Environment
  • Daily Life

What the participants have to do:

Each team must create a presentation of max 8 slides, consisting of the problem, the pre-existing solution faults etc. and your solution.

These are the themes according to which you can find the current problems and come up with a solution. In case you aren't able to think of any, you can find a solution for one of the problem statements given below:

  1. Design a solution to monitor solar panels over a large area with central control and monitoring platform.
  2. Build a service to help students do mock interviews (like interviewing.io). Keep in mind the technical design; incentive for students; incentive for interviewers etc.
  3. Design a platform to manage multiple social media accounts(like Hootsuite). Keep in mind the technical design; subscriptions; monetizations etc.
  4. Suggest a solution to reduce traffic congestion or increase parking efficiency.
  5. Delhi is one of the most polluted cities. Suggest a solution to reduce pollution.
  6. Suggest a solution to tackle the problems associated with mass bunks and missing classes in general.
  7. Many a time your friends drop a reference while talking and you don't know what it means, making you feel left out of the conversation. Suggest a solution to tackle this problem.

SUBMISSION PROCESS

The team must register itself via Google forms and submit the presentation in the same. Thus, each registered team will have submitted their presentations and there won’t be any team with a score of 0 (unless they do something too radical in their presentations )

Link : https://goo.gl/forms/1nR2ayXiNbnUpIOv2

Early submissions fetch brownie🍪 points.
Multiple submissions are not allowed! ❌

SELECTION PROCESS

PHASE I:
The students will form a team among themselves and create a presentation on the problem within 5 days.
The presentation will be submitted via Google forms.
The judges will start evaluating the presentations as soon as they receive them. To reduce the burden on the judging team, we’ll allow early submissions.
After the deadlines, the judges will be given two days extra to wrap up and update the leaderboards.
The top 10 teams will then proceed to the next round.

PHASE II:
The teams that passed phase I will undergo QnA round where they will be asked to pitch their idea and will undergo questioning on many perspectives such as business models, market strategy, any weaknesses etc.
The top 10 will be declared winners and given prizes.

SELECTION CRITERIA

Each submission will be evaluated based on the following criteria…
Originality
Problem Analysis
Practicality

The top 10 teams will be declared the winners (more if some tie-in)

These teams will then undergo a QnA round and the top 2 will be decided from amongst them(more if tied) and declared winners, with the selection dependent upon the completeness of their solution and market strategy.

Remember, you can keep developing your solution even after the first submission...there's always something to improve.
